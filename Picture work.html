Universities of Sindh

Mehran University

Mehran University of Engineering and Technology (MUET) in Jamshoro, Sindh, Pakistan, is a public research university focused on STEM education. It is often referred to as Mehran University or MUET. The university is known for its state-of-the-art laboratories and commitment to research in conventional and emerging fields of engineering, science, technology, and social sciences. MUET aims to promote technological change and sustainable development through higher education, research, and outreach. Key Features and Focus: STEM Focus: MUET emphasizes science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) education and research. Research-Oriented: The university is committed to fostering research and development in various fields, including engineering, science, technology, and social sciences. Technological Advancement and Sustainable Development: MUET aims to contribute to societal progress through technological advancements and sustainable development initiatives. Role Model for other Universities: MUET is striving to become a model for other universities, particularly in terms of engagement with students, communities, industries, and government. Golden Jubilee Celebrations: The university is celebrating its golden jubilee, marking 50 years of its establishment.

Sindh University

The University of Sindh (Sindhi: سنڌ يونيورسٽي, Urdu: جامعہ سندھ) in Jamshoro, Sindh, Pakistan, is a public research university and one of the oldest in the country. It was founded in 1947 and initially functioned as an examining body before relocating to Hyderabad in 1951 and becoming a full-fledged teaching university. The university is known for its research in literature, natural sciences, philosophy, and Sindhology, and is a member of the Association of Commonwealth Universities. Here's a more detailed description: Founding and History: Established in 1947. Initially an examining body. Relocated to Hyderabad in 1951. Became a full-fledged teaching university in Hyderabad. The present campus at Jamshoro, Allama I.I. Kazi Campus, was developed in the late 1950s. Location and Affiliations: Located in Jamshoro, Sindh. Has campuses in Jamshoro, Naushehro Feroze, and Badin. Associated with four law colleges and various other affiliated colleges. Research and Academic Focus: Noted for research in literature, natural sciences, philosophy, and Sindhology. Strives to be a place of higher learning for talented individuals in Pakistan, particularly in Sindh. Focuses on discovering, preserving, and disseminating knowledge. Key Features: One of the oldest universities in Pakistan. Offers bachelor's and master's degree programs. Has a rich cultural and historical heritage. Features technology-based institutions for research and academic excellence. The campus at Jamshoro, named after Allama I.I. Kazi, the first Vice Chancellor, is a landmark. Vision and Mission: To develop human resources through quality education. To cultivate teachers and students who are proud of their culture and committed to society. To contribute to the prosperity, peace, and harmony of the country.
